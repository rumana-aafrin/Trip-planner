Day 1: Rome

Rome, Italy’s capital, is a sprawling, cosmopolitan city with nearly 3,000 years of globally influential art, architecture and culture on display. Ancient ruins such as the Collosseum and the Forum evoke the power of the former Roman Empire.

Since it is the capital city, it’s crowded with peoples all the time. you can visit colosseum in the morning, to avoid crowd. you take entrance ticket and keep going inside. If you wish you can approach guides or else just visit the place with the help of audio in your mobile. You could also visit ancient buildings near colosseum. Relax yourself in the evening.

Day 2: Vatican

Vatican City, a city-state surrounded by Rome, Italy, is the headquarters of the Roman Catholic Church. It's home to the Pope and a trove of iconic art and architecture. Its Vatican Museums house ancient Roman sculptures such as the famed “Laocoön and His Sons” as well as Renaissance frescoes in the Raphael Rooms and the Sistine Chapel, famous for Michelangelo’s ceiling.

Vatican is a holy place. So, try to wear covered clothes, otherwise they won't allow you to see inside places. There are lot of museums in Vatican. You can find a beautiful art of Michelangelo there. Maintain silence in this beautiful holy place.

Day 3: Venice

Venice, the capital of northern Italy’s Veneto region, is built on more than 100 small islands in a lagoon in the Adriatic Sea. It has no roads, just canals – including the Grand Canal thoroughfare – lined with Renaissance and Gothic palaces. The central square, Piazza di San Marco, contains St. Mark’s Basilica, which is tiled with Byzantine mosaics, and the Campanile bell tower offering views of the city’s red roofs.There is also one famous brige called the Rialto bridge. There are lot of shops in the bridge.

This is the dream city to visit for many of us. You wonder how buildings float in the water. Here you visit Piazza di san Marco called as St. Mark’s square for the sunrise. It’s wonderful to see at that time. There are lot of islands and interconnecting bridges in Venice. You should visit Glass bridge. It is made up of strong glass.
